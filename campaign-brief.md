## Brand DNA Summary

FIFO Ready speaks like a mate who's already made it — direct, no-BS, and deeply Australian. The brand is bold-first (8/10) with a casual-to-mid register (4/10 formal), meaning ads should feel like straight talk from the worksite, not a polished sales page. Visually, the brand owns gritty authentic photography: red dirt, hi-vis, real workers — never suits, never stock-photo smiles. The target audience is 22–45-year-old Australians grinding through $50k–$70k/yr work who see $100k–$150k+ FIFO income as a life-changing escape hatch, but don't know where the door is.

## Campaign Concepts

### Concept 1: The Locked Door
**Hypothesis:** The single biggest barrier to FIFO entry is not knowing the process — this concept names that invisible wall directly, then positions FIFO Ready as the key, which should spike relevance scores among cold audiences who've searched "how to get FIFO job" but never found a clear answer.
**Primary Message:** You're qualified enough — you just don't know the process yet.
**Tone:** Casual 4/10, Emotional 6/10, Serious 7/10, Bold 8/10 — direct and slightly confrontational, like a mate calling you out.
**Visual Direction:** A lone worker in hi-vis gear standing outside a mine-site gate at golden hour — gate slightly open, not closed, implying access is right there. Authentic Australian outback light, red dirt foreground, no corporate polish. The gate is the hero; the worker's back is to camera so the audience projects themselves in.
**Target Platforms:** Meta Feed (Facebook) — strong scroll-stop for 28–45 male audience in trade/blue-collar interest segments. Instagram Feed for visual impact.
**CTA:** Get FIFO Ready — $197
**Addresses:** General brand awareness / cold audience pain-point hook (no audit data)

---

### Concept 2: The Numbers Don't Lie
**Hypothesis:** Income contrast is the most visceral motivator for this audience — seeing "$52,000/yr" versus "$130,000/yr" in plain terms triggers the exact aspiration-frustration tension that drives impulse purchases on a $197 offer.
**Primary Message:** Same hours. Same effort. Just the wrong industry.
**Tone:** Casual 4/10, Rational 4/10 (balanced — data anchors the emotion), Serious 7/10, Bold 8/10 — punchy, factual, no fluff.
**Visual Direction:** Split visual energy: left half communicates grinding low-wage work (tradie van, grey suburban street, flat light); right half communicates FIFO abundance (plane on tarmac, red-dirt sunrise, worker striding with purpose). The contrast does the selling. Amber (#F59E0B) used as a dividing glow between the two worlds.
**Target Platforms:** Meta Reels/Stories — the vertical split composition is native to full-screen formats and rewards the 3-second thumb-stop. Also strong in Facebook Feed for the 35–45 age bracket.
**CTA:** Start Earning More — Get Access
**Addresses:** General brand awareness / income aspiration angle (no audit data)

---

### Concept 3: The Fear Removal
**Hypothesis:** Drug tests, medicals, and "no experience" are the three objections that kill purchase intent before the audience even considers price — a concept that names each fear and dismantles it will out-convert generic aspiration ads among people who are interested but hesitant.
**Primary Message:** The medical. The drug test. The interview. We prep you for all of it.
**Tone:** Casual 4/10, Rational 5/10, Serious 7/10, Bold 7/10 — reassuring but not soft, like a mentor who's been through it.
**Visual Direction:** A confident worker in full PPE gear walking toward a waiting charter plane on a sunlit tarmac — posture relaxed and ready, not anxious. The scene communicates "I've got this" without any text in-frame. Warm Australian outback palette, authentic gritty photography, no staged corporate environment.
**Target Platforms:** Meta Feed and Instagram Feed — this concept works in static image format where bullet-style copy can accompany the image. High-intent audiences in 25–40 bracket who've shown FIFO-related interest signals.
**CTA:** See What's Inside — $197 AUD
**Addresses:** General brand awareness / objection-handling angle (no audit data)

---

### Concept 4: The Mate's Testimony
**Hypothesis:** At $197 with no brand recognition, social proof is the fastest trust-builder available — a single real-feeling testimonial framing the transformation ("was doing concreting at $58k, now FIFO at $127k") removes price objection and risk anxiety simultaneously.
**Primary Message:** He didn't have FIFO experience either. Now he's on $127k.
**Tone:** Casual 4/10, Emotional 7/10, Serious 6/10, Bold 7/10 — peer-to-peer, not brand-to-consumer. The voice is the customer's, not FIFO Ready's.
**Visual Direction:** Authentic portrait-style photography of a real-looking bloke (30s, hi-vis, slight smile, outback site in background) — the kind of photo a mate would post on Facebook, not a polished brand shoot. Raw and credible. Warm #F59E0B amber accent used as a subtle framing device rather than a dominant color block. Subject faces camera directly — direct eye contact builds trust in Feed placements.
**Target Platforms:** Facebook Feed primarily — the demographic (30–45, blue-collar male) over-indexes on Facebook and responds strongly to peer-testimonial formats. Instagram Stories for secondary reach with younger 22–30 segment.
**CTA:** Get the Same Playbook — $197
**Addresses:** General brand awareness / social proof / price objection removal (no audit data)
